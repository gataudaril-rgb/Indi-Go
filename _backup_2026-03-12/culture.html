Regional characteristics and demography of India (from the ebook)

AT A GLANCE

Key characteristics and people of India

This page summarizes climate-related regional characteristics and demography as written in the ebook.

Regional characteristics

Temperature

Temperatures vary from cold in the mountains to hot in deserts and lowlands.

Seasons

Influenced by monsoon winds: a rainy season and a dry season.

Winds

The southwest monsoon brings rain, while the northeast monsoon brings the dry season.

Humidity

Coastal areas and regions with frequent rainfall have high humidity.

Demography

Ethnicity / tribes

India is the most populous country in the world, reaching more than 1.47 billion people in early 2026. It is ethnically diverse, including Scheduled Tribes such as Bhil, Gond, Santhal, and Mizo.

Religion

The majority follow Hinduism (~79.8%), followed by Islam (14.2%), Christianity (2.3%), Sikhism (1.7%), Buddhism, and Jainism.

Livelihoods

India is an agrarian society and a major producer of milk, legumes, and tea. Its economy is also driven strongly by services (61.5% of GDP) and industry.

President

President: Droupadi Murmu.

Currency

Currency: Indian Rupee (INR).
