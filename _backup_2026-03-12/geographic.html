Geographic Conditions of India

Click each section to see the full explanation.

Area

India covers about ±3.28 million km², making it one of the largest countries in the world.

Boundaries

  • Land borders: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
  • Sea borders:
    • West: Arabian Sea
    • East: Bay of Bengal
    • South: Indian Ocean
  • Airspace boundary: Adjacent to the airspace of neighboring countries.

A. Geographic Location

India is located in South Asia and forms a peninsula that extends into the Indian Ocean.

B. Geological Setting

India lies at the collision zone between the Indo-Australian Plate and the Eurasian Plate. This formed the Himalayas and makes some regions prone to earthquakes.

C. Latitude & Longitude

India lies between 8°N–37°N and 68°E–97°E. Astronomically, this gives India a tropical to subtropical climate range.

A. Mountains and Ranges

The Himalayas lie in the north, home to some of the highest mountains in the world.

B. Hills

Hilly regions are common along the Western Ghats and Eastern Ghats.

C. Plateau

The Deccan Plateau spans much of southern and central India.

D. Lowlands

The Indo-Gangetic Plain is a fertile region that supports agriculture and dense settlements.

A. Temperature

Temperatures range from cold in the mountains to hot in deserts and lowland plains.

B. Seasons

India's climate is strongly influenced by monsoons, creating distinct wet and dry seasons.

C. Winds

The southwest monsoon brings rain, while the northeast monsoon typically brings drier conditions.

D. Humidity

Coastal areas and high-rainfall regions generally have high humidity.

A. Ethnicity / Tribes

India is the world's most populous country, with more than 1.47 billion people in early 2026. Ethnically, India is often described as being dominated by Caucasoid groups (especially the Indid type). India also has very high tribal diversity (Scheduled Tribes), including Bhil, Gond, Santhal, and Mizo.

B. Religion

The majority of the population practices Hinduism (around 79.8% based on the latest census, and still dominant through 2026), followed by Islam (14.2%), Christianity (2.3%), Sikhism (1.7%), Buddhism, and Jainism.

D. President

India's current president is Droupadi Murmu.

E. Livelihoods

India has a large agrarian population that depends on agriculture, and it is among the world's leading producers of milk, pulses, and tea. However, the economy is also strongly driven by services (61.5% of GDP) and industry.
